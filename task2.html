Penguin - An Overview

Introduction

Penguins are flightless birds that live mainly in the Southern Hemisphere. They are best known for their black-and-white appearance and their ability to swim extremely well.

Habitat

  • Penguins are found in Antartica, South America, South Africa, Australia, and New Zealand
  • Most penguins live in cold regions, but some species live in moderate climates

Physical Characteristics

  1. Penguins have short wings called flippers
  2. Their body is streamlined for swimming
  3. They have dense feathers that keeps them warm
  4. Average height ranges from 30 cm to 120 cm, depending on the species

Diet

  • Penguins mainly eats:
    1. Fish
    2. Krill
    3. Squid
  • They hunt in the ocean and can dive deep to catch food
